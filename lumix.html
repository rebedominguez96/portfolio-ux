Key Insights

What research revealed about the user-money relationship

01

Users avoid finances when stressed

Financial review happens reactively, not preventively — only when a problem already exists.

02

Spending creates confusion, not regret

Users don't understand why they make certain financial decisions. The issue is comprehension, not guilt.

03

Notifications increase anxiety

Constant alerts create a sense of surveillance rather than understanding.

04

Financial data lacks narrative

Users see numbers but can't understand what story those numbers tell about their behavior.

Product Strategy

Three design principles that shaped every decision

We positioned Lumix as a financial wellbeing tool, not just a spending tracker. This shifted the product from monitoring to understanding.

👁️

Emotional visibility of spending

Translate financial data into understandable behavioral signals.

💭

Reflection at the moment of spend

Introduce micro-moments that help interpret financial decisions.

💬

Feedback that reduces financial guilt

Replace punitive alerts with contextual information that fosters understanding.

Design Decisions

Designing for interpretation, not monitoring

Patterns over transactions

Instead of listing transactions chronologically, the product prioritizes behavioral patterns and spending trends to help users see the bigger picture.

Contextual signals over alerts

Traditional notifications were replaced with interpretive signals that explain what each expense means within the user's overall behavior.

Reduced cognitive friction

The interface prioritizes visual clarity and quick reading — allowing users to understand their financial status in seconds.
