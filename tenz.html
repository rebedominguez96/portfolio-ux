Context

From street culture to digital platform

TENZ Performance Club is a sports community built around a clear idea: performance is not only physical — it is also mental. As it grew in Madrid, the challenge became how to structure that experience digitally while preserving the culture that makes it valuable.

🏃

Community Growth
Started as an offline sports community in Madrid with organic word-of-mouth traction.

⚠️

Missing Structure
The experience lives across events and social media without a clear digital system.

🚀

Product Opportunity
A platform could help users discover, join and sustain participation in the community.

How Might We

Design a platform that allows people to discover TENZ, join the community and sustain discipline through structured participation?

Research Insights

What drives discipline in community sports

62.5%

Group energy is the most valued aspect of training

73%

Actively seek sports communities to stay consistent

⏱️

Time defines discipline — structuring training time separates occasional from committed

User Frictions

Barriers that prevent sustained participation

01

Performance gap between participants

Different fitness levels create uneven experiences within the same session.

02

Social pressure to keep up

Users feel discouraged when they can't match the group's pace, leading to dropout.

03

Difficulty finding time to train

Inconsistent schedules make it hard to build a regular training habit.

These frictions reveal the need for a structured system that adapts to different users while maintaining community cohesion.

Product Opportunity

A platform that structures community, not just content

Structure the TENZ community

Give the offline experience a digital backbone — sessions, schedules, member profiles.

Reduce friction in participation

Make discovering, joining and committing to sessions as seamless as possible.

Connect users with brand culture

Embed TENZ's philosophy of discipline and resilience into every touchpoint of the platform.

Key Learnings

What this project confirmed

Community drives commitment more than individual motivation ever could.

01

Community over motivation

Group accountability and shared energy sustain habits far more effectively than solo discipline.

02

Structure reduces friction

Clear schedules, easy sign-ups and predictable formats lower the barrier to consistent participation.

03

Digital amplifies offline culture

A well-designed platform doesn't replace the in-person experience — it makes it more accessible and scalable.
